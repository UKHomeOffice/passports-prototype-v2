British nationality

{{/header}} {{$content}}

When you apply for a first UK passport, we check your claim to British nationality.

In most cases your claim depends on where and when you were born, as well as your parents' circumstances.

There are 6 types of British nationality – British citizenship is the most common. You can check if you're a British citizen online.


Where were you born?

In the UK

Born before 1 January 1983

In most cases you'll be a British citizen if you were born in the UK before 1 January 1983.


Born on or after 1 January 1983

You won’t automatically be a British citizen if you were born in the UK. Your claim to nationality also depends on your parents' circumstances.

If you were born before July 2006 and your parents weren't married or in a civil partnership, you can only claim nationality through your mother or parent 1.

To claim British citizenship, 1 of your parents at the time of your birth must have been either:

  • a British citizen
  • settled in the UK

‘Settled’ means you can stay in the UK without any time restrictions, for example you have indefinite leave to remain.

In a British colony or overseas territory

Born before 1 January 1983

In most cases you'll be a British citizen if you were a citizen of the UK and Colonies (CUKC) on 31 December 1982 and had right of abode in the UK.

'Right of abode' means you:

  • are entirely free from UK Immigration Control and don’t need permission from an Immigration Officer to enter the UK
  • can live and work in the UK without restriction

Born on or after 1 January 1983

In most cases you'll be a British citizen if:

  • you were born in a British overseas territory (except for Akrotiri and Dhekelia military bases in Cyprus)
  • either of your parents were a British citizen when you were born

If you were born before July 2006 and your parents weren't married or in a civil partnership, you can only claim nationality through your mother or parent 1.

Outside the UK

Born before 1 January 1983

You can only claim British citizenship through your father or parent 2, who must have been either:

  • born in the UK
  • naturalised or registered at the time of your birth

Your parents must have been married or in a civil partnership at the time of your birth, or at any point after that.


Born on or after 1 January 1983

You can only claim British citizensip through your parents. They must have been either:

  • born in the UK
  • naturalised or registered at the time of your birth

If you were born before July 2006 and your parents weren't married or in a civil partnership, you can only claim nationality through your mother or parent 1.


Further information

Check the rules if your circumstances are different, or you think you may be eligible for another type of British nationality.
