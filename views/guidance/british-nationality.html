British nationality

{{/header}} {{$content}}

When you apply for a first UK passport, we have to work out your claim to British nationality.

In most cases your claim depends on where and when you were born, as well as your parents' circumstances.

You can check if you're eligible online, or read the rules.


Born in the UK

Born before 1 January 1983

You're a British citizen if you were born in the UK before 1 January 1983.

Born on or after 1 January 1983

You won’t automatically become a British citizen if you were born in the UK. Your claim also depends on your parents' circumstances.

At the time of your birth, one of your parents must have been:

  • a British citizen
  • settled in the UK

‘Settled’ means you can stay in the UK without any time restrictions, for example you have indefinite leave to remain.

If this was your father or parent 2, your parents must have been married or in a civil partnership at the time of your birth, or at any point after that.


Naturalised or registered

You'll automatically become a British citizen if you've naturalised or registered through the Home Office.


Born outside the UK

Born before 1 January 1983

You can only claim British nationality through your father or parent 2, who must either have been:

  • born in the UK
  • naturalised or registered at the time of your birth

You parents must have been married or in a civil partnership at the time of your birth, or at any point after that. If they weren't, you won't have a claim.

Born on or after 1 January 1983

You can only claim British nationality through your parents. They must either have been:

  • born in the UK
  • naturalised or registered at the time of your birth

If this was your father or parent 2, your parents must have been married or in a civil partnership when you were born, or at any point after that.


Further information

If your circumstances are more complicated, you can get more information about other types of British nationality and whether you're eligible.
