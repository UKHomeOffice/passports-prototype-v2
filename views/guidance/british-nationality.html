British nationality

{{/header}} {{$content}}

When you apply for a first UK passport, we check your claim to British nationality.

In most cases your claim depends on where and when you were born, as well as your parents' details.

There are 6 types of British nationality – British citizenship is the most common. You can check if you're a British citizen online or read the rules.


Born in the UK

Born before 1 January 1983

In most cases you'll be a British citizen if you were born in the UK before 1 January 1983.


Born on or after 1 January 1983

You won’t automatically become a British citizen if you were born in the UK. Your claim also depends on your parents' circumstances.

At the time of your birth, either or both of your parents must have been:

  • a British citizen
  • settled in the UK

‘Settled’ means you can stay in the UK without any time restrictions, for example you have indefinite leave to remain.

If you're claiming nationality through your father or parent 2, your parents must have been married or in a civil partnership when you were born, or at any point after that.


Born in a British colony or overseas territory

Born before 1 January 1983

In most cases you'll be a British citizen if you were born in a British colony and had right of abode in the UK.

'Right of abode' means you:

  • are entirely free from UK Immigration Control and don’t need permission from an Immigration Officer to enter the UK
  • can live and work in the UK without restriction

Born on or after 1 January 1983

In most cases you'll be a British citizen if:

  • you were born in a British overseas territory
  • either of your parents was a British citizen when you were born

If you're claiming nationality through your father or parent 2, your parents must have been married or in a civil partnership when you were born, or at any point after that.


Born outside the UK

Born before 1 January 1983

You can only claim British citizenship through your father or parent 2, who must have been either:

  • born in the UK
  • naturalised or registered at the time of your birth

You parents must have been married or in a civil partnership at the time of your birth, or at any point after that.


Born on or after 1 January 1983

You can only claim British citizensip through your parents. They must either have been:

  • born in the UK
  • naturalised or registered at the time of your birth

If this was your father or parent 2, your parents must have been married or in a civil partnership awhen you were born, or at any point after that.


If you were adopted

Born before 1 January 1983

You'll be a British citizen if both the following apply:

  • you were adopted in the UK
  • your adoptive father or parent 2 (or your adoptive parent if it was a sole adoption) is a British citizen

Born on or after 1 January 1983

If you were adopted in the UK, you’ll be a British citizen if either of your adoptive parents is a British citizen – regardless of where you were born.


Further information

Check the rules if your circumstances are more complicated, or you think you might be eligible for another type of British nationality.
