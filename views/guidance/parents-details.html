About your parents

{{/header}} {{$content}}

We need to ask for details about your parents when you apply for a first UK passport. In most cases we use this information to work out your claim to British nationality.

It's important you provide as much information as you can. If you don't know their details, you'll need to tell us why.


If you don't know whose details to provide


Single parents

Provide as much information as you can and leave the details for the other parent blank. You'll need to tell us why you don't know these details.


Same-sex parents

The person who gave birth or who is listed second on adoption certificate should enter their details as parent 1.

The person listed first on the birth or adoption certificate should enter their details as parent 2.


If you were adopted

Give the details of your adoptive parents. We don't need to know your biological parents details.

If it was a sole adoption, give the details of your adoptive parent and leave the details for the other parent blank.


If you were conceived through sperm donation

You don't need to tell us if you were conceived through sperm donation and born in the UK. Give your parents' details as they appear on your birth certificate.

There are different rules if you were conceived through sperm donation and born outside the UK.


If you were born through surrogacy

Provide the details of your parents as they appear on your parental order.

You need to check the rules if you were born outside the UK and don't have a parental order.
