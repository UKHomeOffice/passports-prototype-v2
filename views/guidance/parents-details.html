About your parents

{{/header}} {{$content}}

We ask for parents' details to work out your claim to British nationality and to confirm your identity.

It's important you provide as much information as you can about them. If you don't know their details, you'll need to tell us why.


Single parents

Provide as much information as you can and leave the details for the other parent blank. ou'll need to tell us that you don't know your other parent.

Same-sex parents

Enter the details of the person who gave birth or who is listed second on adoption certificate, under parent 1.

Enter the details of the person listed first on the birth or adoption certificate under parent 2.

If you were adopted

Give the details of your adoptive parents.

If you only have one adoptive parent, give their details and leave the section for the other parent blank. You'll need to tell us it was a sole adoption.

If you were conceived through sperm donation

You don't need to tell us if you were conceived through sperm donation and born in the UK. Give parents' details as they appear on your birth certificate.

There are different rules if you were conceived through sperm donation and born outside the UK.

If you were born through surrogacy

Provide the details of your parents as they appear on the parental order.

You need to check the rules if they don't have a parental order and you were born outside the UK.
