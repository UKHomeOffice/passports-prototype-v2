Documents to prove responsibility for the child

{{/header}} {{$content}}

If you have been appointed in a will as guardian to a child (testementary guardian)

Send the following:

  • documentary evidence to show that you have been appointed guardian
  • a signed and dated letter to confirm that there are no court orders, and that no other person has parental responsibility (in Scotland the letter doesn't need to confirm parental responsibility)

If you're caring for a child in the absence or death of the parent or guardian (loco parentis)

Send the following:

  • statutory declaration

And 1 of the following:

  • their parent's death certificate or will
  • a letter from social services
  • the applicant's full birth certificate
  • proof that you receive their child benefit payments
  • a letter from a head teacher at the child's school, or the child's doctor (they must personally know the child, the applicant and the circumstances)

If you're a foster parent

Send the following:

  • that they consent to the child having a passport
  • they include details of the section of the Act the child is in care under
  • there are no other causes for concern with the application

Other situations where you're acting as a parent

Send the following:

  • a letter explaining the circumstances, and why you're making the application
  • documentary evidence to prove responsibility for the child
