Travelling to EU countries if there’s no Brexit deal

{{/header}} {{$content}}

Advice for British passport holders on how to check if your passport will meet the new rules that will apply for travel to many European countries from 30 March 2019 if the UK leaves the EU without a deal

Check your passport validity before you travel.

When the UK leaves the European Union (EU) there will be new rules for British passport holders travelling to the 26 countries in the Schengen area You’ll need to check whether your passport meets the new requirements when travelling from 30 March 2019.

EU countries which are members of the Schengen Agreement will treat UK passport holders as third country nationals from 30 March 2019 if the UK leaves the EU without a deal. This means they will not accept some UK passports that are nearing their expiry date.

Travel by British passport holders to all countries that are part of the Schengen area will be affected.

There may also be new rules for British passport holders travelling to EU countries that are not part of the Schengen area

More details are in the passports technical notice.

Schengen area travel rules for UK passports

Schengen area countries require that passports held by a third country national must have been issued within the last 10 years, and must have at least 6 months validity left on the day of arrival in the Schengen area.

If there’s no deal, UK passports will be affected by these rules from 30 March 2019.

Adult passports

Your passport should be no older than 9 years and 6 months on the day you arrive in the Schengen area.

Check your passport validity before you travel.

Check the issue date of your passport (not the expiry date).

The issue date should be no more than 9 years and 6 months before the date you intend to arrive in the [Schengen area](#Schengen-countries).

For example, if you plan to travel on 30 March 2019, your passport’s issue date must be on or later than 1 October 2009.

If this is not the case, you will not be able to travel unless you renew your passport.

It’s likely that more people than usual will be renewing their passports – so if you are affected and will be travelling to a country in the Schengen area in spring or summer 2019 you should renew your passport as soon as possible.

Child passports

Check your child's passport validity before they travel.

Check the expiry date of your child’s passport. It must be at least 6 months after the date you plan to arrive in the [Schengen area](#Schengen-countries).

For example, if your child is travelling on 30 March 2019, their passport must have an expiry date on or after 1 October 2019.

If your child’s passport will not meet these rules on the day they arrive in the Schengen area, you must renew their passport.

It’s likely that more people than usual will be renewing their child’s passport – so if your child is affected and they will be travelling to a country in the Schengen area in spring or summer 2019 you should renew their passport as soon as possible.

Travelling to the Republic of Ireland

The rules for UK passport holders travelling to the Republic of Ireland from the UK will not change when the UK leaves the EU. You can continue to use your British passport to travel from the UK to Ireland up until your passport expires.

Travelling to European countries outside the Schengen area

The European countries that are not in the Schengen area are Romania, Bulgaria, Croatia and Cyprus. You should check the rules for the country you’re travelling to as usual.

Using your passport for identification purposes within the UK

You’ll be able to use your passport for identification purposes in the UK until its expiry date.

Crown dependency or Gibraltar passport holders

If you hold a Crown Dependency (Guernsey, Jersey and Isle of Man) or Gibraltar passport you’ll need to comply with the new rules.

If your passport does not meet the new rules, you should renew your passport before you travel.

You can apply for a new passport at your passport office:

  • Gibraltar
  • Guernsey
  • Isle of Man
  • Jersey

UK Overseas Territory passport holders

Holder of other British Overseas Territory passports must already comply with the Schengen Borders Code rules. You should check the rules for the country you’re travelling to as usual.
