Travel to Europe after Brexit

Contents

  • Overview
  • Check a passport for travel to Europe
  • List of countries affected
  • Applying for a new passport

Overview

The rules for travel to Europe will change if the UK leaves the European Union with no deal. The new rules will apply to passports issued in the UK, Gibraltar, Guernsey, Isle of Man and Jersey.

To travel to many countries in Europe after 29 March 2019:

1. You will need to have 6 months left on your passport from your date of departure. This applies to adult and child passports.

2. Any extra months added to a 10 year passport will not count. This only applies to adult passports.

Many adult passports are valid for more than 10 years because extra months used to be added.

Check a passport for travel to Europe

Before booking travel, you need to check your passort meets the new rules. You can use this tool to check a passport for travel to Europe.

List of countries affected

The new rules will apply to countries in the Schengen area. You will need to check your passport before travel to and between:

  • Austria
  • Belgium
  • Czech Republic
  • Denmark
  • Estonia
  • Finland
  • France
  • Germany
  • Greece
  • Hungary
  • Iceland
  • Italy
  • Latvia
  • Liechtenstein
  • Lithuania
  • Luxembourg
  • Malta
  • Netherlands
  • Norway
  • Poland
  • Portugal
  • Slovakia
  • Slovenia
  • Spain
  • Sweden
  • Switzerland

The new rules do not affect travel to Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus or Romania. You should check the entry requirements for these countries.

Travel to the Republic of Ireland

You will be able to travel to the Republic of Ireland until your passport expires.

Applying for a new passport

If your passport is valid for less than 6 months, you'll need to apply for a new passport before you travelling to the countries affected.

More people than usual will be applying for a new passport – make sure you apply with plenty of time before you travel.

If you need to, you can apply to get a passport urgently.
