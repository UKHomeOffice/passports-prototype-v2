Travel to Europe after Brexit

Contents

  • Overview
  • Check a passport for travel to Europe
  • List of countries affected
  • Applying for a new passport

Overview

The rules for travel to Europe will change if the UK leaves the European Union with no deal.

After 29 March 2019, your UK passport will need to be valid for at least 6 months to enter most countries in the European Union.

This will apply to adult and child passports issued in the UK, Gibraltar, Guernsey, Isle of Man and Jersey.

Check a passport for travel to Europe

Before booking travel to Europe after 29 March 2019, use this tool to check a passport for travel to Europe.

For many adult passports, this is not as simple as checking the expiry date. In the past, when you renewed an adult passport you could carry over up to 9 months.

For example, if the expiry date in your passport is September 2019 but you carried over 4 months you need to take off the extra months. Your expiry date for travel to Europe would be May 2019.

List of countries affected

The new rule will apply to travel to and between:

  • Austria
  • Belgium
  • Czech Republic
  • Denmark
  • Estonia
  • Finland
  • France
  • Germany
  • Greece
  • Hungary
  • Iceland
  • Italy
  • Latvia
  • Liechtenstein
  • Lithuania
  • Luxembourg
  • Malta
  • Netherlands
  • Norway
  • Poland
  • Portugal
  • Slovakia
  • Slovenia
  • Spain
  • Sweden
  • Switzerland

You will be able to travel to the Republic of Ireland until your passport expires.

This rule does not affect travel to Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus or Romania. You should check the rules for these countries.

Applying for a new passport

If your passport is valid for less than 6 months, you'll need to apply for a new passport before you travelling to the countries affected.

More people than usual will be applying for a new passport – make sure you apply to renew your passport at least 3 weeks before you travel.

If you need to, you can apply to get a passport urgently.
