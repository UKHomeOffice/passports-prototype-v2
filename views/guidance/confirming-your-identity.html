Confirming your identity online

{{/header}} {{$content}}

For some kinds of applications, you'll need to ask someone to confirm your identity. You may also need to do this if there's a problem with your photo.

This helps protect your identity and confirm the application belongs to you.

How it works

If you've applied with a digital photo, someone can confirm your identity online. They don't need to sign a printed photo.

  1. After completing your application, you'll sign in to check who can do this and provide their details.
  2. We'll send them an email with a link to go online. They will need their UK passport details and your date of birth.
  3. They will see your digital photo and answer a few questions about how they know you. It takes about 10 minutes.

If there's a problem or they don't do this, you'll need to ask someone else. You can only ask one person at a time.

Who can do this

You need to ask someone who lives in the UK and:

  • has known you personally for at least 2 years, for example as a friend, neighbour or colleague
  • and works in or is retired from a a recognised profession
  • and has a current UK passport

They must not be related to you, in a relationship with you or living at the same address. They must not work at Her Majesty's Passport Office.
