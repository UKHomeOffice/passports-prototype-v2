Get someone to confirm your identity

{{/header}} {{$content}}

You'll need someone to confirm your identity if you're applying for a:

  • first passport
  • replacement of a lost or stolen passport
  • renewal of a passport for a child aged 11 or under
  • renewal of a passport if your appearance has changed so much you can't be recognised from your existing passport

Who you can ask

They must:

  • have known you personally for at least 2 years, for example as a friend, neighbour or colleague
  • work in or be retired from a recognised profession
  • live in the UK
  • have a current UK or Irish passport

They can't be:

  • related to you by birth or marriage
  • be in a relationship with you
  • live at your address

What they have to do

We'll ask you to print out a form that has your details on it.

The person you ask to confirm your identity needs to check the details on the form are correct and sign it.

By signing the form they are confirming that:

  • they've known you for more than 2 years
  • you're who you claim to be
  • as far as they know, all the information you've put on the form is correct

We may contact them for more information. Your application may be delayed if they're not available, for example they're on holiday.
