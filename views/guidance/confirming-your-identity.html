How to confirm someone's identity online

{{/header}} {{$content}}

For passport applications with digital photos, the applicant can ask someone to confirm their identity online.

HM Passport Office will tell the applicant if we need to confirm their identity. It isn't necessary for all applications.

What the applicant needs to do

The applicant needs to ask someone who meets the rules to confirm someone's identity.

They must contact the person before giving their contact details to HM Passport Office. They need to sign into their account to enter the person's name and email.

The person they ask must be able to confirm the applicant's address and date of birth, including the year.

If the applicant is a child, the person they ask must be able to confirm where the child was born as well as their parents' names and years of birth.

What the person confirming their identity needs to do

The person confirming the applicant's identity will receive an email from HM Passport Office. The email includes a link and a reference to sign in online.

They will need to:

  1. Enter the reference from the email and the applicant's date of birth
  2. Provide their UK passport details and address
  3. Answer a few questions about how they know the applicant
  4. Confirm some of the applicant's details
  5. Check a passport photo

This takes about 10 minutes. They don't need to sign a printed photo.
