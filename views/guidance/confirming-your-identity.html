Confirming your identity online

{{/header}} {{$content}}

For some kinds of applications, you need to ask someone to confirm your identity. This confirms an application belongs to you and helps protect your identity.

The person you ask can do this online. They don't need to sign a printed photo.

Who can do this

You need to ask someone who:

  • is 18 or older
  • and lives in the UK
  • and has a current UK passport
  • and works in or is retired from a recognised profession

You must have known them personally for at least 2 years, for example a friend, neighbour or colleague.

They must not be related to you, in a relationship with you or living at the same address. They must not work at Her Majesty's Passport Office.

How it works

After submittng your application, you'll need to contact the person and sign in to provide their details.

Her Majesty's Passport Office will send them an email with a reference and a link to go online. They will need your date of birth to sign in.

We'll check their identity, show them your digital photo and ask a few questions about how they know you.

If there's a problem or they don't do this, you'll need to ask someone else. You can only ask one person at a time.
