Confirming your identity

{{/header}} {{$content}}

You'll need someone to check your photo looks like you and confirm your identity if you're applying for a:

  • first passport
  • replacement of a lost or stolen passport
  • renewal for a child aged 11 or under
  • renewal of a passport that expired some time ago

It's an important part of protecting your identity. It helps us confirm the passport application belongs to you.


How it works

After you've completed your application details and paid, we'll ask you to provide the details of someone who can confirm your identity.

We'll send an email to the person you choose to do this, containing a link to go online. They'll need to enter their UK passport details to log in.

We'll ask them to check your photo and confirm it looks like you. We'll also ask them to confirm some details about you.

We'll send you an email after they've confirmed your identity. If there's a problem or they don't reply, we'll ask you to nominate someone else.


Who you can ask

The person you ask must work in or be retired from a recognised profession and:

  • have known you personally for at least 2 years, for example as a friend, neighbour or colleague
  • live in the UK
  • have a current UK passport

They can't be in a relationship with you, live at your address or be related to you by birth or marriage.
